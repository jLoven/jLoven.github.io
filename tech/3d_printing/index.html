3D printing projects

PUBLISHED ON SEP 1, 2013

Zu

I invented “zu”, a 3D printed item to keep your earphones from tangling. It can hold any earphone size and fits in your pocket!

Here’s an 8-second video to show you how zu works.

Plus, zu is printed with quality ABS plastic and can withstand heavy use.  

 

 

 

I created zu at Y-Hack, a hackathon at Yale. I brought my 3D printer (Solidoodle 2) and spent the allotted 24 hours prototyping in SketchUp.  

 

 

 

Klasp

I invented “Klasp” as an assistive technology for elderly patients in nursing homes. I volunteered for five years at a nursing home and saw that a lot of the women love wearing jewelry; however, it’s really difficult to operate tiny clasps with arthritic hands. Thus, Klasp was born!  

 

 

 

Sometimes, all it takes to brighten a resident’s day is to be able to wear their favorite jewelry to Bingo night. With Klasp, these women can now enjoy their sentimental pieces for the first time in twenty years.  

 

 

 

I designed Klasp in SketchUp and printed it on my Solidoodle 2. There are a total of four embedded neodymium magnets inside Klasp, making the device strong enough to hold 175 grams of jewelry. The simple snap design makes it perfect for any age!  

 

 

 

“Very, very easy. God, it can’t be any easier than that!”

- Arlene, a resident who wears a pink Klasp
